# PART 1: THE PSYCHOLOGY ENGINE

## Why Facebook Works for This Business

The targeting isn't demographic. It's **behavioral and contextual.**

Facebook knows:
- Who searched "impact windows" in the last 7 days
- Who visited contractor websites
- Who is in the "home improvement" purchase stage
- Who owns a home (homeownership signal)
- Lookalike audiences of people who already converted

You're not interrupting random people. You're interrupting people who are
*already in the buying process* — but haven't been warned yet.

That's the frame. You're not a salesman. You're the warning they needed.

---

## The "More" Button — Your Most Important Real Estate

Facebook/Instagram truncates post copy at approximately 3 lines (125 characters).
Everything after that is hidden behind "...more"

**Every click of "more" is a micro-commitment signal to the algorithm.**
Facebook tracks these. High "more" clicks = high engagement signal = cheaper CPM
= your ad shown to more people for less money.

The goal: Write the first 3 lines so that NOT clicking "more" feels dangerous.

**The Cliffhanger Formula:**
```
Line 1: Alarming fact or relatable situation (stops the scroll)
Line 2: Stakes raised — "what this means for you" (creates dread)
Line 3: The unresolved question / setup for the reveal — CUT HERE
         [... more]
Line 4+: The answer, the mechanism, the call to action
```

The cut must land at maximum tension. If the first 3 lines resolve anything,
you've lost them.

---

## PAS Framework Applied to Impact Windows

**Problem** — They already have the quote. They think they're done.
The problem is they don't know what they don't know.

**Agitation** — The specific, concrete, named risk. Not "you might be overpaying."
Instead: "Your contract doesn't name the brand. That sentence will cost you $4,200."

**Solution** — Not "call me." Instead: "We run the numbers in 60 seconds."
The solution feels effortless. The risk feels enormous. That ratio drives action.

1. Facebook pre-fills name, email, phone — zero typing friction

 You're delivering value before asking for anything.]

```

"And what's the total they quoted you?"
"What county is this in?"
"Have you gotten any other quotes or is this the only one?"
"How soon are you trying to get this done?"
```

**The Insight Drop (4:30–6:30):**
```
"Okay. So for [X] windows in [County] County at [$Y], 
here's what I'm seeing immediately — 

The fair market range for your scope in [County] is [$low]–[$high].
You're [above/at/below] that range.

The thing that concerns me right now is [permit/brand/warranty issue].
I'll tell you exactly why that matters in a second.
But first — did they give you a deadline to sign?"
```

**The Ask (6:30–8:00):**
```
"Here's what I want to do for you.
I can run your full quote through our system 
and have a written report back to you in [same day/tomorrow morning].
It'll show you exactly where you stand, every flag, and the negotiation language.

All I need from you is a photo of the quote — even a bad photo is fine.
Can you text it to me right now at [your number]?

When you have that report in hand, 
every conversation you have with that contractor is different.
You're holding the truth. They know it."
```

---

## Why This Call Works

- You opened with a curiosity trigger ("find the word 'included'") that puts them in discovery mode
- You delivered real value before asking for anything (the county range)
- You named a specific fear (the permit/brand issue)
- You asked for a tiny commitment (text a photo) not a large one (upload to website)
- The close is framed as empowerment ("you're holding the truth") not as a service sale

# PART 6: AD TESTING HIERARCHY

## What to Test First (In Order)

```
TEST 1 — HOOK (most important, affects 80% of performance)
Run A-01 vs B-01 vs B-03
Same budget. First 3 days. Winner = lower CPL + higher "more" click rate.

Static image vs. simple video (text on screen) vs. carousel
Same copy. Same offer. Winner usually surprises you.

TEST 3 — CTA BUTTON TEXT
"Get Quote Review" vs. "Check My Quote Free" vs. "Find Out Now"
Tiny change. Can shift CPL by 20%.

TEST 4 — LEAD FORM LENGTH
3 questions vs. 5 questions
3Q = more leads, lower quality
5Q = fewer leads, higher quality (better for Track 2)

TEST 5 — OFFER FRAMING
"Free AI scan" vs. "Free quote review" vs. "Find out if you're overpaying"
The word "free" can hurt in some demos — feels cheap.
"Review" feels professional. Test both.
```
